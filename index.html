Why Lyrical Science?

We believe that science shouldn't be held back by funding shortages, so we are building a platform to help scientists develop a sustainable research income by bringing their science to the public.

Crafting the Pitch

We help scientists pitch their complex research by coaching them on their pitch and capturing it on beautiful film.

Building Audiences

We share each scientist's pitch with members of the public who are interested in the research and potentially willing to back it.

Ongoing Funding for Science

Members of the public can make ongoing monthly donations to scientists to conduct basic research over the long term.

Free for Scientists

We will charge a maximum 10 percent fee on donations to cover our services and financial transaction costs.

Scientists Speak About Science Funding

My belief is that the gap between "the fundamental skills which an undergraduate degree equips you with” and “what academia and industry need of an employee” is getting increasingly wider… Hence, there is a need for PhDs to “bridge that gap.” What people can then do, once they have their PhD, that is the tricky part. For me, I wanted autonomy, academic freedom, to pursue ideas which I believed could be useful, fascinating, or revolutionary. I don't believe we should equip students to face the realities of the job market; we should change the system in order to best utilise the hordes of amazing scientists we produce. So much talent gets squandered, simply through lack of opportunity, and once again, lack of funding.

James Bowen

Lecturer in Materials Engineering
The Open University (United Kingdom)

Science funding is highly competitive, especially for early career scientists (graduate students and postdoctoral fellows). The majority of early career researchers depend on their lab funding. Few are successful in obtaining independent fellowships, which are mostly reserved for translational research. Although most of the startling discoveries and innovations in the recent years stem from basic science research (e.g. CRISPR-Cas based gene editing), acquiring funding for research with no direct ties to a disease is challenging.

Sonali Majumdar

Research Scholar and Postdoctoral Leader
Sloan Kettering Cancer Center in New York

I’m using medical research and community work to pioneer a new HIV nano-vaccine, and to encourage young people to engage in scientific innovation… As a young scientist, the most crucial challenges I had to face were the absence of a medical research culture for non-degree holders, especially for young students. It resulted in a huge difficulty for me to apply for grants. Now, we can apply for government and some private sector grants. However, the waiting time is too long to proceed because we lack seniority and paper qualifications and it often delays the valuable inputs.

Rakitha Malewana

Researcher in HIV
Student at American National College in Sri Lanka
