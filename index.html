Why Lyrical Science?

We believe that science shouldn't be held back by funding shortages, so we are building a platform to help scientists develop a sustainable research income by bringing their science to the public.

Crafting the Pitch

We help scientists pitch their complex research by coaching them on their pitch and capturing it on beautiful film.

Building Audiences

We share each scientist's pitch with members of the public who are interested in the research and potentially willing to back it.

Ongoing Funding

Members of the public can make ongoing monthly donations to scientists to conduct basic research over the long term.

Free for Scientists

We will charge a maximum 10 percent fee on donations to cover our services and financial transaction costs.

Building the Human Voice Box

Nazia Mehrban
University College London

Nazia engineers tissue for patients who have lost organs due to illness or accident. She is currently working with surgeons to recreate the human voice box to help patients suffering from throat cancer and related illnesses recover their ability to speak, swallow and cough.

Scientists Speak About Science Funding

I wanted autonomy, academic freedom, to pursue ideas which I believed could be useful, fascinating, or revolutionary. I don't believe we should equip students to face the realities of the job market; we should change the system in order to best utilise the hordes of amazing scientists we produce. So much talent gets squandered, simply through lack of opportunity, and once again, lack of funding.

James Bowen

Lecturer in Materials Engineering
The Open University (United Kingdom)
Science funding is highly competitive, especially for early career scientists. The majority of early career researchers depend on their lab funding. Few are successful in obtaining independent fellowships… Although most of the startling discoveries and innovations in the recent years stem from basic science research (e.g. CRISPR-Cas based gene editing), acquiring funding for research with no direct ties to a disease is challenging.

Sonali Majumdar

Research Scholar and Postdoctoral Leader
Sloan Kettering Cancer Center in New York
When you complete a PhD, you have the highest qualification in the land. You are elated, you are proud. Then come the postdoctoral positions and you feel you're making a difference and your research matters… You apply for funding and lay out your soul on the application form… You spend 6 months to a year putting together the various documents required for the application and then you get an email stating "we are holding emergency meetings due to Brexit." The implication is clear. These words mean "It is likely funding will be cut." This is what happened to me and I can only describe it as heart-breaking.

Nazia Mehrban

Postdoctoral Researcher, Surgery and Interventional Science
University College London
Typically, only 5-10 percent of grants submitted to government agencies are funded. There is a big gap in funding that would normally help PhDs and post-doctoral researchers transition to the next level of their careers… Most researchers do not have access to philanthropic and venture capital based funding resources. Despite the creativity and innovation that research revolves around, the pool of financial support for it is miniscule compared to other sectors such as media, entertainment and sports.

Jayeeta Basu

Assistant Professor, Neuroscience and Physiology
NYU Neuroscience Institute
